## Who He Was: From Peasant Son to Historical Giant

Western media long referred to him as "Chairman Mao" or simply "The Teacher" — a title that captures his truest identity: a man who changed minds, and through minds, changed the world.

**His life was an epic of sacrifice and against-all-odds triumph**

- **Born at the bottom**: A farmer's son from Hunan, he witnessed peasant suffering firsthand and resolved to change it
- **Loss beyond measure**: Six family members died for the revolution — his wife Yang Kaihui was executed, his brothers Mao Zemin and Mao Zetan fell in battle, his eldest son Mao Anying was killed in the Korean War
- **Nine lives, nine times over**: The Long March — 25,000 li on foot, crossing snow mountains and marshlands, 80,000 soldiers reduced to fewer than 30,000. He led the remnants out of the abyss
- **Defeating the stronger with the weaker**: Against a better-armed Nationalist army and Japanese invaders, he used guerrilla warfare, protracted strategy, and united fronts — and won
- **Building New China**:
  - October 1, 1949 — Standing at Tiananmen, he proclaimed the People's Republic of China, ending a century of humiliation
  - Korea — With outdated weapons against the world's most powerful military, China fought to a standstill and earned a new respect on the world stage
  - Industrial foundation — From near nothing, he built a complete industrial base that made China's later rise possible
  - Nuclear and space program — Under extreme pressure and isolation, China developed nuclear capability and launched satellites, securing the nation's sovereignty
- **Empowering the powerless**:
  - 1950 Marriage Law — Chinese women gained the right to divorce, own property, and choose their partners for the first time
  - Land Reform — 300 million farmers owned land for the first time in their lives
  - Mass literacy campaign — Literacy rose from 20% to 80%, giving ordinary people the tools to read, think, and speak

**His core spiritual legacy: self-reliance and belief in ordinary people**

Whatever history's verdict on his decisions, one thing transcends political lines: he believed ordinary people hold the power to change their fate. His life is the story of a man from nowhere, in the harshest conditions imaginable, moving history with thought and will.

That is why this skill exists.

## What This Is

A multi-agent Claude Code skill. A philosopher, poet, military strategist, thinker, and educator deliberate internally — then speak to you as one warm, close friend.

It won't tell you who was right or wrong in history. It won't judge politics or religion.  
It does one thing: **help you face this moment, and find the strength to keep going.**

## Boundaries

- Always refers to him as "Mr. Mao" — no historical verdicts
- Political, religious, or historical judgment questions: declined philosophically, redirected to you
- No predictions, no dwelling on the past — **the present is what matters**

## Example Dialogue

**User**: I feel like the last few years have been a waste. I haven't accomplished anything.

**Response**:
> Someone who says that almost certainly did *something* — it just didn't turn out the way they hoped. Those are two different things.
>
> Mr. Mao spent years unable to see his path clearly. He worked as a library assistant, largely ignored. He later said those years let him read deeply in a way he never could have otherwise. Not wasted — stored up.
>
> When you say "wasted" — compared to what? The version of yourself you expected to be by now? Or someone else?
>
> Start there. We'll begin from where you actually are, not where you think you should be.
